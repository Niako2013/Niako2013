Khachapuri is from georgia.
Here's the recipe:For the dough, you will need all-purpose flour, salt, sugar, dry yeast, warm milk, and olive oil or melted butter.
For the filling, use shredded mozzarella cheese, crumbled feta cheese or traditional sulguni cheese,
and an optional egg for extra richness.

In a bowl, mix warm milk, sugar, and yeast and let it sit until foamy.
Add flour, salt, and olive oil, then knead until smooth and elastic.
Cover with a towel and let it rise until doubled in size.
Preheat your oven to four hundred degrees Fahrenheit.
Mix the cheeses together in a bowl and if using an egg, mix it in for extra creaminess.
Roll out the dough into a circle, place the cheese mixture in the center, and fold the
edges over, pinching them together at the top.
Flatten gently with your hands to create a thick round pie.
Transfer to a baking sheet and bake until golden brown.
Brush with butter when hot and let it rest before slicing.

Enjoy your homemade Imeretian Khachapuri with a warm cup of tea or any Georgian dish.
